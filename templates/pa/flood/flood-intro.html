8-Hour Flood & Homeowner's Insurance Update for Public Adjusters

This course fulfills the state-mandated requirements for the Public Adjuster Law and Policy continuing education component for Florida-licensed public adjusters. (3-20)

In this course we will review some of the fundamental concepts and some of the complex coverage details involving flood insurance, FEMA, and other programs related to property risks. We will review how the water versus wind issue was addressed by the Florida Supreme Court in the Florida Farm Bureau v Jernigan. This course also provides an introduction to homeowners insurance, focusing on different types of coverages and liabilities. In addition, we will also provide answers for the most common questions and important concepts.

Our course is 100% online and can be taken in the privacy of your own home at your convenience.

Class highlights include:

  • The Role of FEMA
  • Increased Cost of Compliance Coverage
  • Damages and Property Not Covered
  • Claims Handling Procedures
  • Other Issues Affecting Flood Coverage
  • Property coverages, such as the dwelling, other structures, personal property, and loss of use
  • Covered perils for the dwelling, other structures, and personal property
  • General property exclusions
  • Property conditions
  • Liability coverages, including exclusions and conditions

This course has been approved by the Florida Department of Financial Services for insurance continuing education.
